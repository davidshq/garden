# Think Differently

## Introduction
This section includes materials which have impacted the way I think, particulary by challenging or informing my understanding of the world.

## Cautionary Note:
These works have been read over a period of 30+ years, many of them had an impact on me but I cannot vouch for their accuracy after so many years. If you find any contain misinformation that impacts the worth of the work significantly, please let me know.

## The Civil War Trilogy (Jeff Shaara)
A three-volume novelization of the American Civil War. It needs to be read alongside Jeff Shaara's novels on the American Revolution and as such provides some interesting insight into character and behavior during both conflicts.

## No Man Knows My History (Fawn M. Brodie)
A biography of Joseph Smith, founder of Mormonism. Provides fascinating insights into the relatively recent development of a religion and the historical context in which Mormonism arose. I find it most useful in regard to these two areas. Helping me understand:
1. How can a religion come into being?
2. What was the world like in the 19th century?

## The Last Disciple Series (Hank Hanegraaff and Sigmund Brouwer)
Offers a preterist view of the apocalyptic scriptures in the CHristian New Testament through a novelization. Challenges popular (Left Behind) evangelical thinking on the meaning of the Book of Revelation, etc.

## Uppity Women of Medieval Times (Vicki León)
Short biographical entries on various amazing women who lived and did things during the male-dominated medieval ages.
