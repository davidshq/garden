When someone gives you brilliant feedback on something you've worked hard to build it can feel like, "I must be such a dunce, how did I not see that?"

And sometimes when you see what someone else has built you may think, "That is absolutely awful. How could they build something so poor? If they had just done a, b, c, and d it would be awesome!"

Remember that building something from scratch is often much harder than refining something. When we refine something we are building on what someone else has already accomplished.

What is the practical meaning of this principle?

1. Don't think you are so amazing when you are able to give valuable feedback.
2. Be humble when giving your feedback.
3. Don't feel like a dunce when someone else shows you ways you can improve something tremendously.
