Get friends, make friends, keep friends.

I cannot overemphasize the importance of a supportive community to living a healthy, happy life.

Find people you enjoy, who challenge you, teach you, who let you challenge and teach them, who enjoy you. Find people with common interests and values.

But don't let this be the only friends you have. Look also for friends who experience and think about the world differently from you. Let them expand the richness of your mind and experience.

Invest time and energy into your friends. Learn what they like and love, support them in it. Ask them about it.

Make the effort to keep strong friendships alive. Sometimes this can be very challenging for us, I know I've not done great at it. It is something I wish I was better at.

And don't make only a single really good friend, make multiple really good friends. You don't have to have ten or twenty, but a few, is really, really good.

Life happens and sometimes people have to leave our lives for different reasons - maybe they move away, maybe they die, maybe there is a fight we just can't seem to get over, or maybe our interests just diverge. In any case, don't make yourself rely on a single friendship - make sure you have a few.

And make multiple friends with the gender you are romantically attracted to. Having multiple friends of that gender may help you avoid falling hard for one that wouldn't be a good match for you.
