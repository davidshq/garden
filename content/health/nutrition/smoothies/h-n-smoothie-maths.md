# Introduction

For some of us *friction* can cause insurmountable obstacles in accomplishing our objectives. I'm always looking for ways to reduce the amount of *friction* involved in making smoothies.

Two ways I'm attempting to do this are:

1. Knowing exactly how much of each ingredient I'll need for a single batch (52x 12 oz mason jars) so I can purchase the appropriate quantities.
2. Prepping and freezing fresh ingredients so that I the batch process itself is quicker.[^3]

This document attempts to provide a guide to the math required to figure this out.
# IMPORTANT: On Blending Leafy Greens
I've given my blenders a good beating by attempting to blend too many leafy greens at the same time. Leafy greens require a high ratio of water to leafy greens in order not to jam up the blender.

In addition, the leafy greens need to be added in small batches to the blender and it is safer to do less, dump it into the storage container and do another round rather than trying to fill the blender.

I found 13 oz of frozen spinach that had been microwaved to still be a hefty load for my Vitamix - and I was parceling it in probably 1/4th at a time.

Please keep this in mind when viewing leafy veggies. While I'm giving total amount I'm blending, this is being blended across multiple "runs" and the water is likewise being divided evenly between the runs.

# Ingredients

## Bananas
- 14x + 1 cup water = 64 fl oz[^4]
- Per Banana: ~4.6 oz
## Lemons
- 13x + 2 cups water = 64 fl oz
- Per Lemon: ~ 4.9 oz
## Avocados
- 10x + 3 cups water = 64 fl oz
- Per Avocado: ~6.4 oz
## Kale
- 1x 12 oz frozen chopped kale (microwaved) + 2 cups (16 oz) water = 20 oz
- 1x 16 oz fresh kale (washed in hot water, microwaved for 2 mins) + 4-5 cups (32 - 40 oz) water = 6 cups
## Spinach
- 2x 13 oz frozen spinach (microwaved) + 4 cups water = 48 oz
## Cauliflower
- 2x 12 oz frozen cauliflower (microwaved) + 3 cups water = 40 oz
## Blackberries
- 16 oz frozen blackberries + 3 cups water = 4.5 cups
- 16 oz frozen blackberries (microwaved for 3 mins) + 1 cup water = 3 cups
## Mushrooms
- 4x 8 oz mushrooms + 2 cups water = 7 cups
## Strawberries
- 64 oz (4 lbs) of frozen strawberries (microwaved for 2 mins) + 4.5c water = 12 cups
## Raspberries
- If one does them frozen one needs 4-5 cups of water per 24 oz of raspberries and results in 5.25 cups.
- 24 oz frozen raspberries (microwaved for 2m) = 4.75 cups.
- Total this batch: 10c raspberries
## Blueberries
- 48 oz (3 lbs) of frozen blueberries (microwaved for 2 mins) + 2c water = 7.9 cups
# What You Actually Get Making a Smoothie Batch
Theoretically 64 oz (blender jar) divided by 12 (size of mason jar) is 5.33 but I generally get 4.25 - 4.75 smoothies from a 64 oz batch.

There are multiple factors that cause this: (1) I may overfill some smoothie jars, (2) things take up less volume after being blended, (3) I don't attempt to get every last drop out of the blender.

**The takeaway is that 64 oz from a blender fills roughly 4.25 12 oz mason jars.**

This means to make enough smoothies to fill 51 mason jars I need to make 12 batches in the blender.[^1]
