As someone with ADHD friction can be a major obstacle to completing tasks/projects.

What is friction? Its anything that slows down the process, that adds complexity, that one finds unpleasant, etc. The more friction there is the less likely one is to complete the task.

I've run into a number of friction points in my journey to create healthy smoothies. This document outlines various friction points and how I have resolved them.

# Friction: Frequency

While making smoothies isn't an unpleasant experience it also isn't a super exciting experience. If I had to do it frequently it would become a major friction point. Here are a few areas in particular that I find friction in:
## Dishes

Yes, we have a dishwasher. I still don't like doing dishes. If I can avoid using dishes I will but dishes are inevitable when making smoothies.
## Cleaning

My OCD may be showing a bit but I like to do a thorough cleaning of my work area. This includes wiping everything down with clorox wipes (counter tops, knobs on cabinets, handles for refrigerator, door knobs, deep freezer, phone, laptop, etc.).
# Friction: Food Prep

One is supposed to cook frozen vegetables. While I didn't do this for months and survived, I would be the person who ends up getting sick from doing so...and I want this to be a process others can do, and I don't want to recommend unsafe processes.

In addition, there is the process of cutting up various ingredients - particularly lemons and avocados.

For avocados and bananas there is the added complexity of keeping them until they are ripe and then ensuring one cuts them up before they go bad. This hasn't kept me from making smoothies in the long-run, but it has frequently resulted in me postponing making smoothies (and running out) or in having to throw away produce that goes bad.

# Solution 1: Batching Smoothies

Instead of making a few smoothies at a time I make 50+ at a time. To be able to do this I:

1. Purchased a deep freezer I can keep the smoothies in.
2. Purchased lots of mason jars (and plastic lids).
3. Buy the ingredients in large batches.
4.  Purchased a large blender (72 oz).

This means that I only need to do dishes, do a deep clean, etc. every few weeks instead of every few days.
# Solution 2: Batching Fresh Ingredients

Up to this point I've been timing my creation of making smoothies to when the fresh ingredients are still fresh and those that need to ripen have ripened.

This also means that when I make smoothies I need to prep the fresh ingredients. This isn't a big deal but it is a bit messy (avocados and lemons) and adds significant time to each batch.

To fix this I've purchased 6 1/2 gallon mason jars and I'm going to begin processing the fresh ingredients when they are ready, entirely separate from making the smoothies, and then freezing the results.

This decouples the fresh ingredients and their ripening from making the smoothies - for those who are into coding, you'll recognize loose coupling as a positive improvement. ;-)
